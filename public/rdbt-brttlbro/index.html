Route Brief

137 Miles of Quintessential Vermont

A 137-mile hardtail loop starting and ending in Brattleboro, VT. Designed by Joe Cruz for bikepacking.com ‚Äî this v2.1 variant threads through the Green Mountain National Forest, behind Stratton Mountain on the legendary IP Road, and through some of Vermont's most beautiful hill villages.

The character changes as you ride: the first day rolls through open valley farmland; the middle two days dig into remote forest doubletrack and rocky pitches; the final day brings you back through pastoral hill country and the village of Grafton.

Where We Sleep

The Camping Plan

Three nights, three different situations. Winhall Brook is at risk ‚Äî we call it the morning we leave. Hapgood Pond is confirmed and reserved either way.

Night 1

Dispersed Camping ‚Äî Harriman Reservoir Area

National Forest dispersed camping near the reservoir or on GMNF land shortly after the Catamount Trail section. No reservation needed, no fee.

Water is available from reservoir tributaries and streams ‚Äî filter everything. Find a site off the doubletrack in the trees.

Night 2 ‚Äî Primary Target (If Open)

Winhall Brook Campground

The classic Night 2 stop on the original route ‚Äî right on the line, on the banks of the West River, with a great flow from the IP Road day. Closed since the July 2023 Vermont floods due to bathroom damage.

Status as of early 2026: closed, possible reopening pending construction. We're monitoring it. Decision will be made the morning of departure ‚Äî if it's open, we go to Winhall. If not, Hapgood Pond is confirmed and ready.

Night 2 ‚Äî Confirmed Backup

Hapgood Pond Campground, Peru VT

A legitimate USFS campground in the Green Mountain National Forest near Peru, VT. Pit toilets, fire rings, and a swimming pond. 8 miles further than Winhall Brook but a genuinely excellent campground.

Already reserved on recreation.gov. If Winhall is closed, this is where we sleep ‚Äî no scramble, no stress.

Night 3 ‚Äî Option A (Recommended)

Mollie Beattie State Forest, near Grafton

A gem of a primitive camp right on the route ‚Äî free, officially permitted, and in a beautiful CCC-era forest east of Grafton. Groups under 11 need no permit. Hike bikes in from Gate 2 parking area.

Important: The old CCC stone house is strictly off-limits for camping. Camp in the designated tent sites in the forest proper. Howe Brook is your water source. Call the district office to confirm current access before the trip.

The Grafton Inn

The classic New England inn in the heart of Grafton village. A proper bed, hot shower, and the inn's own pub with excellent food ‚Äî the only bit of luxury on the trip. Book well in advance for summer dates.

üíß Water Strategy

Bring a reliable filter (Sawyer Squeeze or BeFree). Vermont streams are abundant throughout the GMNF sections but assume everything needs filtering. The IP Road has multiple stream crossings. In drought conditions, the early sections near Harriman can run low ‚Äî fill when you see water.
